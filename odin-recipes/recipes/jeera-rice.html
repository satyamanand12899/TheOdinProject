Butter Chicken

Description

Jeera rice (lit. cumin rice) is a traditional accompaniment to many Indian curries. Recipe from Veg Recipes of India Image from Whisk Affair.

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice 200 g
  • Water 1 L
  • Bay leaf 1 leaf
  • Ghee (clarified butter) 1 tbsp
  • Whole cumin seeds 1 tbsp
  • Green chili 1 pc
  • Cloves 2 pc
  • Green cardamom 2 pc
  • Black cardamom 1 pc
  • Cinnamon 1/2 in

Procedure

  1. Rinse basmati rice until the water is clear. Soak rice for at least 30 minutes then drain the water.
  2. Pour water into a pot and add the whole spices except cumin and green chili. Raise to a boil and gently add the rice. Bring to a simmer immediately after. Leave to simmer without a cover as per cooking instructions.
  3. Once the grains reach the desired tenderness, strain and rinse the rice using a colander to stop the cooking process.
  4. Heat ghee in a small pan. Add cumin and green chili. Stir until the cumin crackles and/or green chili blisters.
  5. Add the cumin and green chili to the warm rice and gently mix with a fork.
  6. Insert immersion blender OR cool the sauce and transfer into a kitchen blender. Blend until smooth then strain the sauce into a pot.
  7. Stir in the cream, sugar and kasoori methi. Adjust thickness by adding water, if needed. Once warm, transfer the chicken into the pot. Cover the pot and let simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with curry of your choice.
