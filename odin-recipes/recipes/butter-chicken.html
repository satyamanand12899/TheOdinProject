Butter Chicken

Description

Butter chicken is a North Indian dish created in the 1950s in the Moti Mahal, New Delhi when a chef decided to stir the leftover tandoori chicken in a rich buttery tomato gravy. Recipe from Cafe Delites Image from Cooking Classy.

Ingredients

For the chicken,

  • Boneless, skinless chicken 800 g
  • Greek yoghurt 1/2 cup
  • Minced garlic 3/2 tbsp
  • Minced ginger 1 tbsp
  • Salt 1 tsp
  • Ground cumin 1 tsp
  • Ground turmeric 1 tsp
  • Red chili powder 1 tsp

For the sauce,

  • Crushed tomatoes 400 g
  • White onions 300 g
  • Heavy cream 300 g
  • Olive oil 250 g
  • Ghee 2 tbsp
  • Minced garlic 3/2 tbsp
  • Minced ginger 1 tbsp
  • Sugar 1 tbsp
  • Garam masala 3/2 tsp
  • Ground cumin 3/2 tsp
  • Ground coriander 3/2 tsp
  • Ground turmeric 3/2 tsp
  • Kasoori methi 1 tsp
  • Red chili powder 3/2 tsp
  • Salt To taste

Procedure

  1. Combine the chicken with its listed ingredients to marinate for at least 30 minutes (preferably overnight),
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet at medium-high heat; once sizzling add chicken in small batches to prevent crowding. Fry chicken until golden brown with mild charring (approx. 5 minutes each side). Set aside on a plate.
  3. Heat the ghee in the skillet and, once sizzling, fry onions for 5 minutes. Scrape the browned bits left on the skillet while sweating the onions.
  4. Add the garlic and ginger pastes, saut√© for a minute then add powdered spices (coriander, cumin, garam masala, chili powder). Stir until well incorporated and fragrant.
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes and salt. Gently mash the tomatoes as the sauce thickens for around 15 minutes.
  6. Insert immersion blender OR cool the sauce and transfer into a kitchen blender. Blend until smooth then strain the sauce into a pot.
  7. Stir in the cream, sugar and kasoori methi. Adjust thickness by adding water, if needed. Once warm, transfer the chicken into the pot. Cover the pot and let simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro and cream. Serve hot with jeera rice or naan bread.
